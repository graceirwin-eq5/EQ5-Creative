## Market Positioning: The Three-Lane Framework

```
Lane 1: PROGRAMMATIC HCP ("White Coat")      → DeepIntent ($637M investment)
Lane 2: ENDEMIC / EHR ("Point-of-Care")      → PulsePoint, Doceree
Lane 3: SOCIAL HCP ("Blue Jeans")            → Equals 5 (only player)
```

**Core positioning:** "DeepIntent owns programmatic HCP. PulsePoint owns endemic/EHR. We own social HCP. It's a three-lane market, and we're the only company in our lane with 100% deterministic NPI-level capability."

**Why social matters:** Your physicians spend 10-15x more time on social media than on any endemic medical channel. We're the only platform that can reach them there with verified, NPI-level precision — and report back at the physician level.

---

## vs. DeepIntent

**Their lane:** Programmatic HCP targeting in clinical/endemic settings ("white coat")
**Our lane:** Social HCP targeting on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok ("blue jeans")

### Universal Talk Track:
"DeepIntent is strong in programmatic, but can they run NPI-targeted campaigns on Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok? Can they report back which specific physicians saw your ads on social? We can — and we outperformed them head-to-head at Amgen. The client confirmed it."

### When Asked About DeepIntent's $637M:
"It validates our market. They raised $637M to dominate programmatic HCP. But programmatic is the 'white coat' — reaching doctors in clinical settings. We own the 'blue jeans' — reaching the same doctors on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and TikTok, where they spend 10-15x more time. Two different lanes, and they can't enter ours."

### For Specialty / Rare Disease (small HCP universes):
"For specialty pharma — CNS, immunology, rare disease — your HCP universe is often a few hundred to a few thousand physicians. Programmatic scatter isn't efficient. You need 100% deterministic, NPI-level targeting on the channels those specialists actually use. We delivered that for 65 rare disease brands."

---

## vs. PulsePoint

**Their lane:** EHR and endemic/in-workflow HCP engagement
**Our lane:** Social channels where HCPs spend time outside the clinic

### Universal Talk Track:
"PulsePoint owns the EHR and endemic space. We own the social space. Your HCPs spend 10-15x more time on social media than any endemic channel. We're complementary — or we're the better standalone choice for social HCP campaigns."

### For Oncology / Specialty:
"PulsePoint is endemic and EHR focused — solid for in-workflow engagement. But oncologists scrolling Instagram at 9 PM or cardiologists on LinkedIn during lunch? That's our lane. And we report back at the NPI level from those social channels."

## vs. Doceree

**Our lane:** Social channels where HCPs engage after hours

**Our lane:** NPI-level deterministic targeting on social

## vs. CMI Media Group (as competitor, not partner)

**Context:** CMI is both a major partner ($3.5M revenue) AND a competitive threat when they handle social HCP internally.

### When CMI Is the Competitor:
"CMI is a great agency partner — they grew 147% year over year with us. But when they run social HCP in-house, they're using Meta's native tools without our NPI datagraph. Our 87% list match rate and physician-level reporting isn't something you can replicate with standard agency media buying."

### Pricing vs. CMI ($65 CPM claim):
"That $65 CPM only covers the PLD component. When you add audience data, media spend, and account management, their all-in Meta CPM is ~$140. Ours is $185, and that includes dashboard access, sFTP delivery, CRM integrations, custom FIA coordination, and user-level reporting. The gap is $45, not $120 — and that $45 covers significantly more value."
